Costco

Closing the Gap Between Costco's Buying Power and Last-Mile Fulfillment Efficiency

Costco operates on a deceptively simple model: sell high volumes of a tightly curated SKU count at margins thin enough to keep members coming back. That model works — until it doesn't. With over 850 warehouses globally and a supplier base that hasn't been structurally re-evaluated against the post-pandemic freight landscape, the operational assumptions baked into Costco's supply chain are showing stress in places the P&L doesn't immediately surface.

The Problem

What we observed

Costco's treasure-hunt merchandising depends on a supply chain that can absorb sudden, high-volume shifts in product mix — seasonal items, Kirkland Signature expansions, and opportunistic buys from suppliers. What we found is that the vendor compliance and inbound logistics coordination layer hasn't scaled proportionally with Costco's e-commerce fulfillment growth, particularly around bulky and big-ticket items shipped direct to members. The gap between warehouse-optimized replenishment cycles and the demand variability introduced by Costco.com is creating quiet inventory imbalances — items arriving late to floor, overshoots on pallet allocations, and supplier lead times that were calibrated for a pre-2020 freight environment. This isn't a visibility problem. It's a structural mismatch between how Costco buys and how it now needs to deliver.

Our Solution

What we'd do

We are proposing a focused 6-week supply chain diagnostic and vendor alignment audit targeting Costco's top 40 direct-import and bulky-goods suppliers. The deliverable is a ranked gap analysis — scored by fulfillment risk, lead time variance, and compliance deviation — paired with a renegotiation playbook that regional merchandising and logistics teams can act on without an overhaul of existing supplier contracts. We will also produce a one-page prioritization matrix mapping which supplier relationships carry the most downstream risk to in-warehouse availability versus which are cosmetic inefficiencies. The output is decision-ready, not advisory.

Expected Impact

What changes

Based on comparable work in high-volume, low-margin retail environments, a targeted audit of this scope typically surfaces 8–14% in recoverable freight and compliance cost within the analyzed supplier cohort. More importantly for Costco's model, it reduces the probability of empty-floor events on anchor SKUs — the kind of stockouts that don't just lose a sale but erode the membership value proposition that drives renewal rates above 90%.
